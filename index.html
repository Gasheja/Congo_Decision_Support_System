Democratic Republic of Congo

Explore the living standards of people in the different regions of the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

The Democratic Republic of the Congo (DRC) struggles to meet its citizens' basic needs due to a lack of data-driven decision-making.
Health care is inadequate, and disease outbreaks are frequent because of insufficient planning data.
Education suffers from low literacy rates and imbalanced teacher distribution due to missing school and student data.
Many people lack clean water and sanitation because proper planning data is unavailable.
Infrastructure development, including roads, transportation, and electricity, is limited by a lack of data on population needs.
Conflicts and displacement issues are worsened by the absence of data on ethnic groups and conflict zones, affecting aid efforts.
Corruption thrives without transparent data on government spending, and regular, accurate population counts are missing, leaving significant gaps in understanding demographic needs.
Improving data collection and utilization is crucial for the DRC government to make informed decisions and enhance services for its citizens.
